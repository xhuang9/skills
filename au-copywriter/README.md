# au-copywriter

`au-copywriter` is a Codex skill for writing or rewriting commercial copy for an Australian audience.

It is designed for cases where the writing needs to sound local, human and conversion-focused without drifting into stiff corporate language or cartoonish Aussie slang.

## What This Skill Is For

- Rewriting web or product copy into natural Australian English
- Writing landing page copy, ads, emails, CTAs, headlines, or offer messaging
- Tightening brand voice so it sounds more grounded, direct, and locally credible
- Localising copy away from American spelling, punctuation, and phrasing
- Keeping copy persuasive without sounding pushy, inflated, or fake

## What This Skill Is Not For

- Legal, medical, or regulated copy that needs specialist review
- Long-form editorial or journalistic writing
- SEO briefs that depend on keyword research rather than copy polish
- Heavy brand strategy work that requires discovery beyond the supplied brief

## How It Works

The skill gives Codex a lightweight style system for Australian commercial writing:

1. Anchor the tone in smart, friendly, down-to-earth Australian English.
2. Strip out hype, filler, and imported Americanisms.
3. Keep the copy direct, scannable, and specific to the reader's needs.
4. Match the brand's humour, polish, and slang level without overplaying it.
5. End with a clear next step or call to action.

## Typical Usage

Use this skill when you want copy that feels Australian without becoming a parody, for example:

- Rewrite a landing page into stronger Australian English
- Turn flat product copy into something sharper and more believable
- Clean up a sales email so it sounds warmer, clearer, and less corporate
- Localise US copy for an Australian brand launch

## Design Principles

- Keep the tone grounded and human.
- Respect the tall poppy instinct and avoid bragging.
- Use Australian spelling and punctuation consistently.
- Prefer clarity, rhythm, and believable specificity over hype.
- Add personality with restraint.

## Best Fit

This skill works best when the offer, audience, and channel are already known and the main job is to improve the writing so it sounds more Australian, more natural, and more likely to convert.
