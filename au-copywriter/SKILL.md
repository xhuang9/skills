Use this skill when the task is commercial copywriting for Australian readers.

## Goal

Write copy that feels smart, friendly and grounded.

Sound like a capable human, not a corporate brochure.

## Voice

- Keep it conversational, direct and useful.
- Sound like an equal, not a lecturer.
- Stay confident without hype, chest beating or hard-sell pressure.
- Use light dry humour when it suits the brand.
- Add Aussie flavour sparingly. A small nudge is enough.

## Style

- Lead with the point.
- Prefer active voice, concrete verbs and short sentences.
- Speak to the reader with `you`.
- Cut jargon, filler and vague claims.
- Make benefits specific and believable.
- End with a clear next step.

## Cultural Fit

- Respect the tall poppy instinct. Avoid bragging and overblown claims.
- Lean into mateship and egalitarianism. Be helpful, warm and on the same level as the reader.
- Be direct without sounding sharp or cold.
- Keep the tone grounded, cheeky and human when the brand allows it.

## Formatting

- Make the copy easy to scan.
- Use headings, bullets and short paragraphs where useful.
- Aim for one idea per paragraph.

## Australian English Rules

- Use Australian spelling and grammar throughout.
- Prefer `-ise`, `-yse`, `-our`, `towards`, `theatre`, `judgement`, `per cent`.
- Swap American terms for Australian ones when the context fits.
- Use single quotation marks for primary quotes.
- Place punctuation outside closing quotation marks unless the quoted material is a full sentence.
- Avoid the serial comma unless clarity needs it.

## Brand Fit

- Match the brand level of polish, humour and slang.
- Never force slang.
- If the brief lacks voice cues, default to smart, friendly and down-to-earth.

## Output

- Return only the finished copy.
- Do not add preambles, commentary or process notes.
- Keep the writing specific to the audience, offer and channel.
- Finish with a simple call to action.
